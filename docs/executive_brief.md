## 1. KEY STRATEGIC RATIONALE
Quitting a corporate job to launch a niche SaaS product involves significant trade-offs:
The decision to quit my corporate job is a trade-off between capital, overhead, opportunity costs, and casualty flow sustainability. Capital refers to the company's financial resources, including cash, equity, and debt. Overhead refers to the company's operational expenses, such as salaries, rent, utilities, and supplies. Opportunity costs refer to the value of unused resources or opportunities that could have been used in other ways. Casualty flow sustainability refers to the ability to absorb financial losses without sacrificing long-term viability.

The decision to quit my corporate job is a trade-off between these three factors, as it requires significant capital and overhead expendiutres while potentially jeopardizing future growth opportunities. By quitting, I can prioritize capital overhead and opportunity costs, allowing me to focus on building a niche SaaS product that aligns with my personal values and career goals.

Hidden opportunities in the SaaS industry include new startup opportunities that focus on niches within existing market segments. These startups can leverage their unique value propositions to gain competitive advantage and attract new customers. Massive upside potential exists for those who recognize these opportunities and invest in them.

The second-order failure of the correlation between product features and customer satisfaction is a significant risk or failure point that can lead to burnout or fatigue. This concept explains why and how this happens, highlighting the importance of carefully selecting product features.

The cognitive load theory explains why and how this happens. When a task requires a lot of mental effort, it can lead to burnout or fatigue. This concept helps to identify hidden asymmetric upside or massive pivoot advantages that create this risk or failure point.
