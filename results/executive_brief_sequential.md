### 1. Financial & Capital Overhead Costs  
**Immediate Consequences:**  
- **Salary Sacrifice:** Quitting a corporate job with a $100k+ annual salary immediately removes a stable income stream. If the SaaS product fails to generate revenue within 6–12 months, the individual faces a cash crunch.  
- **Upfront Costs:** Development of a SaaS product requires significant capital. For a niche product, initial costs may include:  
  - Hiring developers (e.g., $50–$150/hour for full-stack engineers, with 200+ hours for MVP).  
  - Hosting and infrastructure (e.g., AWS or Firebase costs, which can escalate to $5k+/month for scalability).  
  - Marketing and sales tools (e.g., HubSpot, Mailchimp, or paid ads).  
  - Legal and compliance expenses (e.g., domain registration, SSL certificates, GDPR compliance).  

**Second-Order Consequences:**  
- **Opportunity Cost of Time:** Time spent building the product could have been used to accumulate equity or side-hustle income. For example, a corporate employee might have earned $50k in side projects during the first year of entrepreneurship.  
- **Debt Accumulation:** If the individual takes on loans or uses personal savings, default risk increases. A 2023 study by the SaaS Capital Fund found that 68% of SaaS startups fail within the first 3 years, with cash flow being the primary cause.  
- **Tax and Retirement Implications:** Self-employment taxes (27.4% in the U.S.) and lack of employer-sponsored retirement plans (e.g., 401(k)) could erode long-term financial security.  

**Third-Order Consequences:**  
- **Long-Term Financial Instability:** If the product fails, the individual may face a prolonged period of unemployment, leading to reduced credit scores, difficulty securing future loans, and potential reliance on family support.  
- **Investor Pressure:** If the individual raises capital, they may face demands for rapid growth or profitability, increasing the risk of burnout or misaligned priorities.

- **First-Mover Advantage:** Early adoption in a niche could create network effects or brand loyalty, making it harder for competitors to replicate.

- **Monetization Flexibility:** SaaS allows for recurring revenue models (e.g., $99/month subscriptions), which can scale with minimal marginal costs. A 2022 SaaS Profitability Report by ProfitWell showed that SaaS companies with 100+ customers often achieve 80%+ gross margins.  
- **Passive Income Potential:** Once the product is built, the individual could monetize through affiliate marketing, partnerships, or licensing, reducing reliance on active management.
